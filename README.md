# Game-Pause-Menu
A game pause menu for DIG2500

What did you make?
I made a pause menu based on rpgs and open word games like Kingdom Hearts and Minecraft
## Intended User or Player
Who is this interface designed for?
This interface would be designed for people who are playing whatever game this menu would be attatched to. It would account for many people such as people who frequently play video games and beginners. 

## User / Player Goal
What should the user or player be able to do or understand?
The player should be able to easily tell what this menu is for, what the buttons do and should be able to easily access stats. 

## Inspiration Interfaces
List the inspiration interfaces you used and briefly explain what you learned from them.
Games such as Kingdom Hearts, Minecraft, and Super Mario Odessy helped inspire my interface. 
Something I noticed within all f them that I initittially found odd was the titles were the smallest portion of the screen. This makes sense in retrospect as this feature is one of the least important features of the pause menu. I also noticed within Kingdom Hearts and Minecraft specifically was that content relatd to the character like skins and stats were stored beneath the character seperate from other visual info and given less importance within the visual hierarchy. This again makes sense as people who are trying to access setting or other game changing elements are given priority over those who are there for more surface level cosmetic purposes.

## Design Choices
- Layout: As is custom with most games, buttons are placed to the left, player and stats to the right, title at the top and legal notices at the bottom.
- Color: I chose a dark background color so that your eyes would immediatley be drawn to the brighter color buttons and stats bar. I also made it so that when buttons are hovered over they are orange as that is the compliment of blue, the background. 
- Typography: For typhography I made it a very simple font that can be easily read by all users. I also made it contrast whatever the backdrop was so darker colors were on lighter backgrounds and vice versa.
- Visual hierarchy: I made it so the buttons would stand out first as they are the largest feature of the menu and are on the left hand side. This was done because buttons will be the feature players interact with the most (the only thing reallly) so they should be the most important within the visual hierarchy. I then made it so the character profile first followed by the stats. This is done so players who are only pausing to view stats can still easily access this information without pulling from the central focus. This is followed by the pause title for anyone who truly isnt in the know and finally the copyright as tis is the least important feature and is only there for legal purposes. 
- Feedback states: For feedback I made it so when the player hovered over a button it became larger and orange. This indiactes to players that this is interactable and makes the selected button visually distinct. 
- Responsive design: When the tab is shrunk images decrease in size and sections are stacked on top of each other for better readability.
